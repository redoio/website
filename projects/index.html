AI Assistant

Our AI search assistant helps attorneys, academic researchers, policymakers, and the general public, easily query our database of prison sentences. Using natural language prompts, users can generate cohorts of individuals that meet the conditions they are interested in. These criteria could be related to age, gender, controlling offenses, prior convictions, sentence length, time served, sentencing county, etc.

Timeline:
March 2025
Beta
Details:

Our models are inherently rules-based, transparent and deterministic. We care deeply about algorithmic bias, and actively work to ensure that our results do not exacerbate existing biases in our society. Our query tool is currently in development and will be available to users for beta testing in February 2025. Please reach out if you are interested in using our tool, and providing feedback.

Encouraging "Second-Look" Case Reviews

In January 2025, the Office of the State Public Defender (OSPD), in collaboration with Redo.io, sent 58 county Public Defenders’ Offices in California, detailed letters with the names of candidates serving time for non-violent, non-serious, and non-sexual offenses. These candidates were identified using our open database of 95,000+ state prison sentences. 

Timeline:
Jan 2025
Complete
Details:

Using our open database of prison sentences we applied a series of selection criteria to find individuals in the California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation (CDCR) system who are sentenced to over 20 years for qualifying offenses, and have served at least half of their sentence. Roughly speaking, we disqualify any homicide offenses, registrable sex offenses, violent felonies, super strike offenses, and other serious felonies. 

Public Defender’s Offices in California have the ability to review these cases, and if suitable, consider them for resentencing under AB700 or PC1172.1. There are limitations, however, with the data we provide. For instance, we cannot tell whether an individual was already considered for resentencing or not. Additionally, since Redo.io’s database of prison sentences was acquired through public records laws, it is possible that some offenses are not disclosed by CDCR due to privacy or other restrictions in the public records law.

Open Data Initiative

Data on the population currently incarcerated in any of the thirty-five state prisons under the jurisdiction of the California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation (CDCR) as of December 2023. Information was acquired via the California Public Records Act (CPRA).

The Department applied several privacy (health information, age, etc.) related exclusionary criteria to release these records to Redo.io. We applied an md5 hash algorithm to further mask the original CDCR identification numbers thereby adding another layer of privacy and security.

Resentencing Eligibility Determination

A data science tool for the Three Strikes Project at the Stanford University School of Law that proactively and automatically identifies candidates in California’s prison system who are suitable for “second-look” case reviews via Prosecutor Initiated Resentencing.

Timeline:
Dec 2023
Complete
Resentencing Eligibility Model

The Three Strikes Project is legal clinical seminar where law students represent individuals serving life sentences for non-violent crimes under California’s Three Strikes Law. Lawyers assist district attorney’s offices throughout the state who are seeking opportunities to safely and effectively reduce California’s prison population through Prosecutor Initiated Resentencing (PIR).

But, given that there are over 100,000 people in California’s prisons, lawyers need efficient and accurate ways to identify those who are eligible for PIR instead of manually reviewing piles of paperwork. Redo.io’s open-source eligibility tool helps lawyers filter their caseload and apply their expertise efficiently. We are currently under a contract with the Stanford School of Law to develop this application.

Our tool

 

Summary

 

A rules-based and deterministic model that generates cohorts of individuals eligible for PIR based on criteria established by district attorney’s offices.

What it is:

  • A deterministic model with a rules-based framework designed by Redo.io in conjunction with legal experts
  • Easily interpretable and explainable for non-technical users and audience
  • Responsive to user requests, i.e. an attorney can request changes to the eligibility criteria and see updated results

What it is not:

  • A blackbox predictive model
  • A decision-maker that determines the cases that are ultimately resentenced by the courts

Execution

 

  • Ingest the demographics, current offenses and prior offenses of individuals in California Department of Corrections and Rehabilitation (CDCR) custody
  • Run the eligibility model (with selection criteria and scenarios designed by legal experts)
  • Return a cohort of individuals eligible for PIR
  • Provide attorneys easy-to-understand personal profiles of each candidate
  • Re-run the eligibility model when new data on the prison population is available
